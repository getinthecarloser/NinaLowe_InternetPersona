And Then There Was Nothing

 

    “The smoke is coming”, she said calmly in a childlike sing song voice. They had been walking for days now. Elliot and Cleo stayed a good distance behind Evan and Nikolai. Evan was the eldest of the four. She never liked her given name, Evangeline; so people that knew her knew to call her Evan. But everyone that knew her had disappeared into the smoke.


“Shut up Cleo, the smoke has been coming ever since we started walking.”


“I’m sorry; I just thought you all should know that it’s moving faster.” Cleo turned back around and caught up to Elliot, her long blonde hair flowing in the wind. She was never one to be phased by Evan’s mean words.


“Well if it’s gaining speed, then we need to gain speed as well, that’s all,” said Elliot.


“This is your fault,” said Nik. “Where are we even going anyway?”


Evan stopped in her tracks. She turned around slowly for intentional dramatic effect. “Oh my god Nik, if you ask that one more time I’ll kick you in the nads!”


The field of tall grass they were walking through kept tickling his ankles and he complained about it every few meters.
