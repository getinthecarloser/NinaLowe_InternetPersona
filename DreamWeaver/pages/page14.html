     The firefighter walked up to the policeman after they had gotten the fire under control. Officer Colby was looking up at the blackened apartment building with his notepad out ready to get the information for the report.


“It’s a tragedy really, four of them died tonight,” said the firefighter. “All of them under 18.” The firefighters were surrounding the building and comforting the ones who got out in time. To the right of Officer Colby was a little girl wrapped in a blanket. She was holding the blanket closed with her Barbie doll in hand.


“How did it start?” asked the officer. The firefighter looked down at the little girl.


“It started in one of the apartments; we have reason to believe it was a stove top fire,” he replied. “It took down one of the walls between the apartments into a young teen’s room. He didn’t survive.” A woman was telling him earlier that she heard an explosion and she rushed to her son’s room but it was too late. He was then told by a young man that the fire spread from that apartment to his own. His brother and sister were visiting for the weekend and they were caught in the smoke.


“May they be in a better place,” said the policeman.
