     Evan, from her position next to Nik in the grassy field, whipped her head around faster this time; her hair once again whipping him in the face, waking him from his reverie.


“Seriously, Elliot! It wasn’t your stupid quilt!”


“Get your freaking hair out of my face.” said Nik. “It’s all I’ve been eating lately.” Elliot looked down at the handful of dead prairie dogs he’d caught. Hair wouldn’t be any worse than the taste of those, he thought.


“Maybe if you didn’t insist on walking so close to me, you wouldn’t get my hair in your mouth,” Evan said. Nikolai’s cheeks heated up as he looked down and coughed quietly into his hand.


“It’s gone again,” said Cleo. She kept checking behind her to see where the smoke was. They didn’t know what it was and she was nervous it would do something unexpected.


“Will you stop that Cleo, seriously we don’t need a play by play,” Evan replied. Cleo lowered her eyes to the ground and started playing with the bottom of her shirt.

“You know we can’t keep running forever,” she whispered quietly, so that only Elliot heard.
